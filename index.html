Languages of the World

Native and Non-Native Speakers of Languages

English and Mandarin stand out as the two most widely spoken languages globally. However, a nuanced insight emerges from the analysis of this graph, revealing a profound contrast in the demographic characteristics of those proficient in these languages. Notably, over half of the world's English speakers have acquired it as a secondary language, distinct from their mother tongue. In stark contrast, almost 90% of Mandarin speakers are native speakers. This data underscores the remarkable prevalence of English as the most widely learned second language worldwide.

Number of Languages Spoken by the Population

While it is noteworthy that 43% of the global population possesses the ability to fluently communicate in two or more languages, the linguistic landscape in Australia presents a distinct perspective. In the Australian context, only 22.3% of the population can be classified as bilingual. This discrepancy highlights both the global trend towards multilingualism and the unique linguistic composition within Australia
